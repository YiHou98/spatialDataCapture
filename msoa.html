Grocery Cluster

This cluster is associated with MSOA areas with high Getis-Ord Gi* scores, which means the areas with many groceries are clusted together. Most of them are located in the inner London.

Food Desrt

This cluster is associated with MSOA areas with low Getis-Ord Gi* scores and the areas with few groceries are grouped. Also, we add the areas with 0 grocery. This cluster can represent the food desert in London.

Common Area

This cluster respresents the MSOA areas which are not in the first two clusters.

Groceries

On this map, we choose Waitrose as a representative of upscale grocery chain, Sainsburys, Tesco and Asda as mid-range groceries, while Aldi and Lidl are the cheapest ones.

Waitrose stores are mostly located in inner London, Tesco and Sainsburys have the largest amounts of groceries across the London compared with other chains, while Asda, Aldi and Lidl are distributed in outer London. The cheap grocery chains are always located in outer London to lower the cost, while Waitrose, whose target audience are middle and upper class, locates the groceries in the inner London to better serve the customers.
